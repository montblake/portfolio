Hi. I'm Blake, a software engineer and web developer based in Chicago, Illinois.

I believe exploring my projects is a good way to get to know me as a developer. I've created a landing page for each which gives some insight into how I view each project, its particular challenges, and what elements I am working on next.

If you're looking for more information about who I am and how I approach the work, you might be interested in my thoughts on being bootcamp grad, what draws me to full stack development, what I did in my previous career, and the focus of my current work.

I got my start in coding with Stanford University's online pandemic offering Code in Place which gave me a foundation in computer science principles, an approach to the deconstruction of problems, a grasp of the fundamentals of the Python language, and an appreciation for the magic of programming.

After that, I continued with a year of self-study focusing on front-end web development. Eventually I found myself getting bogged down with trying to master every detail. Unsure of what was important, I became stuck. For me, bootcamp was the antidote.

My program, General Assembly's Software Engineering Immersive, used a project-focused approach, alternating between periods of learning (lectures, code alongs, problem sets, practical lab problems) and projects. It was in the project weeks that I really learned how things were put together, how to find bugs, break down problems, become comfortable working with code, reusing and recycling things to my purpose. Most importantly, I learned how to trust the iterative nature of the development process, achieving "minimum viable product" and then building out new features as time allows.

We began with JavaScript and moved quickly from frontend to back, building our knowledge first with old school jQuery, then NodeJs and Express, Mongoose and MongoDB, and finally adding React for fullstack application. Once we understood the full stack, we switched to Ruby and the Rails framework as well as changing to a relational database PostgreSQL. Finally we ended with a self-study capstone project during which I returned to my Python roots and tackled the Flask microframework.

This progression gave me the confidence to know that I can tackle any technology necessary and that behind all the various stacks, the same engineering principles are at play.
